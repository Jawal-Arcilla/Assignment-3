Edward Catmull

Timeline

  • Edward was born on March 31st, 1945.

  • In 1972, Edward had animated a version
    of his left hand and it was noticed by a
    Hollywood producer, which later had
    incorporated to the move Futureworld

  • Edward had earned his doctorate in
    computer science and was hired by a
    company called Applicon in 1974.

  • During 1979, Edward had became a
    Vice President at Industrial Light and
    Magic computer graphic division at LucasFilm.


Contributions

  • Co-Founder of Pixar and President of Walt
    Disney Animations Studio.

  • First 3D computer graphic animation.

  • Invented tweening(a process that involves
    adding frames in between two chosen frames)

  • Software for 2D animations that automatically
    produces frames of motion in between two frames.

  • Academy and Technical award from the Academy
    of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences in 1993 and yet
    again in 1996.

  • Awarded the IEEE John von Neumann Medal for
    pioneering contributions to the field of computer
    graphics in modelling, animation and rendering.


Quote


“What makes Pixar special is that we acknowledge we will always have problems, many of them hidden from our view; that we work hard to uncover these problems, even if doing so means making ourselves uncomfortable; and that, when we come across a problem, we marshal all of our energies to solve it. This more than any elaborate party or turreted workstation, is why I love coming to work in the morning. It is what motivates me and gives me a definite sense of mission.”
